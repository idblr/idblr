I co-authored an article in [*Sleep Medicine*](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/sleep-medicine) entitled ["Environmental, Social, and Health Burdens in relation to Sleep-Disordered Breathing among Patients of Community-Based Health Center Clinics in the United States"](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.sleep.2026.108943) led by [Dr. Wensu Zhou](https://orcid.org/0000-0002-4517-5504).   

<!--more-->

We assessed the potential relationships between community-level environmental, social, and health burdens (see the [Environmental Justice Index](https://www.atsdr.cdc.gov/place-health/php/eji/index.html) from the [U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention](https://www.cdc.gov) [CDC] and the [Agency for Toxic Substances and Disease Registry](https://www.atsdr.cdc.gov) [ATSDR]) and sleep-disordered breathing (SDB) prevalence in 2022 across 1,406,727 patients in the [Accelerating Data Value Across a National Community Health Center Network](https://www.pcori.org/research-results/2015/accelerating-data-value-across-national-community-health-center-network-advance) (ADVANCE), led by [OCHIN](https://ochin.org).

We found that higher overall [U.S. Census Bureau](https://www.census.gov) census tract-level environmental, social, and health burdens were associated with higher SDB prevalence with variation by sociodemographic group.
