About Me

Originally from the mountains of Jefferson County, Colorado, I attended the Unversity of Colorado at Boulder studying disease ecology with Dr. Pieter Johnson where I received a Goldwater Scholarship and graduated with highest honors. I then moved to Atlanta, Georgia and received a Ph.D. from Emory University in Environmental Health Sciences with Dr. Lance Waller and funding from the National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellowship Program. I am now a postdoctoral researcher at the National Cancer Institute through the Cancer Prevention Fellowship Program working with Drs. Rena Jones and Mary Ward in the Occupational and Environmental Epidemiology Branch in the Division of Cancer Epidemiology and Genetics.

My research focuses on the spatial and environmental epidemiology of cancer and infectious disease, including novel applications of spatial point pattern analyses. You can follow my research in progress on my GitHub. I have written a scientific publication and have presented my research at numerous scientific conferences. I am an ad hoc reviewer for Cancer Causes & Control, Methods in Ecology and Evoluation, and PLoS - Neglected Tropical Diseases.
